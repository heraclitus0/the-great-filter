## 🌌 What Is This

A pixel art civilization survival game rooted in the Fermi Paradox. You manage a civilization across four fields, investing resources every second, watching your world breathe — and hoping it's enough when the filters arrive.

No tutorials. No hints. No second chances on the same world. Learn through loss.

---

## 🎮 How To Play

<div align="center">

| Field | Represents | Why It Matters |
|:-----:|:----------:|:--------------:|
| 🌾 **Food** | biological survival, population health | feeds your people — but also feeds the plague |
| ⚡ **Energy** | power, industry, infrastructure | powers everything — but excess destroys the climate |
| 🔬 **Science** | research, technology, adaptation | cures disease, builds AI — but needs Society to survive |
| 🤝 **Society** | culture, governance, social cohesion | holds civilization together — more planets, more strain |

</div>

Every second you earn coins. Invest them into whichever fields you think will keep your civilization alive. Decisions are **irreversible**. Once invested, you cannot reallocate.

Six filters arrive across your civilization's lifespan. Survive all six and the universe offers you another planet. Fail any one and your civilization falls — permanently.

---

## 🔴 The Six Filters

<div align="center">

| # | Filter | What It Actually Tests |
|:-:|:------:|:----------------------|
| 1 | 🦠 **The Plague** | Food alone won't save you. Science cures it. Energy distributes the cure. |
| 2 | ☢️ **The War** | Power without wisdom is annihilation. Energy and Society must balance. |
| 3 | 🌡️ **Climate Collapse** | Every neglected field accelerates collapse. No exceptions. |
| 4 | 🤖 **The Machine God** | Science without Society goes rogue. Society without Science stagnates. |
| 5 | 👽 **The Signal** | Something vast is watching. It does not welcome threats or primitives. |
| 6 | ✨ **The Great Filter** | Nobody knows what it tests. The universe decides. |

</div>

Each filter doubles the time before the next arrives — **10 → 20 → 40 → 80 → 160 seconds**. The Great Filter has no timer. No warning. Just darkness, silence, and your numbers floating in the void.

---

## 🪐 Progression

```
Earth  →  survive 6 filters  →  expand to next habitable planet
       →  manage civilization across multiple worlds simultaneously
       →  jump solar systems when current system is exhausted
       →  interstellar  →  intergalactic (mythical)
```

Each new planet **doubles your income** but comes with its own cost profile. Hostile worlds consume that income faster than friendly ones. The corner of the screen shows one number — how many planets your civilization spans. The complexity lives underneath it, felt through economics, never explained through dashboards.

---

## ⚠️ What Makes It Hard

**No filter is static.** Each one runs a dynamic algorithm weighing all four fields against each other and against your planet's natural profile. Spamming one field will kill you — just not always immediately.

**Build surplus.** Early planets give you room. The next planet will demand more. A civilization that barely survived Earth arrives at Mars with nothing in reserve. Nations that stored grain survived famines. Nations that didn't, didn't.

**The information gradient is real.** You know exactly what humanity knows about each world:

<div align="center">

| Planet | What You Know | Uncertainty |
|:------:|:-------------:|:-----------:|
| 🌍 Earth | Everything | Minimal |
| 🔴 Mars | A lot — but black swans lurk | Some |
| 🧊 Europa | Theorized, observed, uncertain | Many unknowns |
| 🟢 Kepler-452b | Distant observations only | Mostly unknown |
| ❓ Alien worlds | Almost nothing | Pure black swan |

</div>

The difficulty curve is not artificial game balancing. It is the actual state of human knowledge.

---

## 💀 Loss & Irreversibility

- Fail a filter on your only planet → **extinction**, run ends
- Another habitable planet exists → your species flees there, lost planet gone forever this run
- Managing multiple planets and fail a filter → weakest planet collapses
- Lose your last planet → **full game over**, begin again
- Lost planets return in future runs — but never in the current one

## 🧠 Philosophy

<div align="center">

*The invisibility in this game is not a design choice for difficulty.*
*It is a philosophical position.*

*We don't know what Mars will truly cost us.*
*We don't know what The Great Filter actually tests.*
*We make our best decisions with what we can observe*
*and the universe decides the rest.*

**You are not supposed to know. Neither were we.**
