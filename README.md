# THE GREAT FILTER

> *most civilizations don't make it.*

## What Is This

A pixel art civilization survival game rooted in the Fermi Paradox.

The Fermi Paradox asks a simple question: the universe is vast and old, so where is everybody? The Great Filter theory answers it — somewhere between single-celled life and an interstellar civilization, something kills you. Most don't make it. Maybe it's behind us. Maybe it's ahead.

This game makes you feel that.

---

## How To Play

You manage a civilization across four fields:

| Field | What It Represents |
|-------|-------------------|
| 🌾 Food | biological survival, population health |
| ⚡ Energy | power, industry, infrastructure |
| 🔬 Science | research, technology, adaptation |
| 🤝 Society | culture, governance, social cohesion |

Every second you earn coins. Invest them into whichever fields you think will keep your civilization alive.

Six filters arrive across your civilization's lifespan — each one a different kind of crisis. Survive all six and you pass The Great Filter. Then the universe offers you another planet.

---

## The Six Filters

| # | Filter | What It Tests |
|---|--------|--------------|
| 1 | 🦠 The Plague | Food alone won't save you. Science cures. Energy distributes. |
| 2 | ☢️ The War | Power without wisdom is annihilation. Energy and Society must balance. |
| 3 | 🌡️ Climate Collapse | Every neglected field accelerates collapse. No exceptions. |
| 4 | 🤖 The Machine God | Science without Society goes rogue. Society without Science stagnates. |
| 5 | 👽 The Signal | Something vast is watching. It does not welcome threats or primitives. |
| 6 | ✨ The Great Filter | Nobody knows what it tests. The universe decides. |

Each filter doubles the time before the next arrives — 10 → 20 → 40 → 80 → 160 seconds. The Great Filter arrives with no timer. No warning. Just darkness.

---

## What Makes It Hard

**No filter is static.** Each one runs a dynamic algorithm weighing all four fields against each other and against your planet's profile. Spamming one field will kill you — just not always immediately.

**Decisions are irreversible.** Once you invest, you cannot reallocate. The civilization that spammed Food and skipped Science earned its Plague death.

**Build surplus.** Early planets give you room. The next planet will demand more. A civilization that barely survived Earth arrives at Mars with nothing in reserve.

**The information gradient is real.** You know what humanity knows. Earth — everything. Mars — a lot, with black swans. Europa — some. Alien worlds — almost nothing. The difficulty curve is the actual state of human knowledge.

---

## Progression

```
Earth → survive filters → expand to next habitable planet
     → manage civilization across multiple worlds simultaneously
     → jump to next solar system when current is exhausted
     → interstellar → intergalactic (mythical)
```

Each new planet doubles your income. Each new planet also has its own cost profile — hostile worlds consume that income faster. The corner of the screen shows one number: how many planets your civilization currently spans. The complexity lives underneath it, felt through economics not explained through dashboards.

Losing a planet while managing multiple worlds ends the run entirely.

---

## Philosophy

This is not a fun arcade game. It is a quiet dread simulator.

The invisibility is intentional. You are not supposed to know what each filter demands. You are not supposed to know what Mars will truly cost you. You make your best decisions with what you can observe and the universe decides the rest. That is not a design choice. That is the truth.

Most civilizations don't make it. The ones that do feel it.
